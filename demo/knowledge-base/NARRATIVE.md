# EstiMate - Company Narrative

## Company Overview
**EstiMate** is a B2B SaaS platform automating estimation processes, communications, and knowledge base operations for construction and consulting firms.

## Timeline: January - June 2024

### Founders
- **Henry**: Co-founder (Marketing, Sales, Accounting)
- **Steven**: Co-founder (Architecture, Customer Success, Finance)

### Employees
- **Sarah**: Customer Success Manager (hired March 2024)

### Clients (10 total)
1. **BuildCorp** - First client (Jan 2024), construction, enthusiastic early adopter
2. **ConsultPro** - Engineering consulting (Feb 2024), demanding enterprise
3. **DesignHub** - Architecture firm (Feb 2024), light user
4. **MegaConstruct** - Large construction (Mar 2024), almost churned in May
5. **TinyBuilders** - Small contractor (Mar 2024), low engagement
6. **PrecisionEng** - Precision engineering (Apr 2024), power user with custom needs
7. **QuickBuild** - Fast-paced construction (Apr 2024), integration challenges
8. **UrbanDesign** - Urban planning (May 2024), unique workflow
9. **InfraWorks** - Infrastructure (May 2024), pilot program
10. **EcoConstruct** - Sustainable building (Jun 2024), latest addition

## Key Story Arcs

### Arc 1: Pricing Evolution (Jan → Apr)
- **Jan**: Launch with flat $99/month pricing
- **Feb**: BuildCorp requests features, realize pricing too low
- **Mar**: Debate between usage-based vs tiered pricing
- **Apr**: Switch to $299/499/999 tiered model
- **May**: MegaConstruct threatens to churn over price increase
- **Jun**: Introduce grandfathered pricing to save MegaConstruct

### Arc 2: Architecture Pivot (Feb → Jun)
- **Jan-Feb**: MVP monolith, working fine
- **Mar**: Performance issues with ConsultPro's large datasets
- **Apr**: Steven proposes microservices, Henry worried about complexity
- **May**: Heated debate, decide to optimize monolith first
- **Jun**: Successfully optimized, defer microservices to Series A

### Arc 3: Feature Prioritization Conflicts
- **Feb**: BuildCorp wants PDF export, ConsultPro wants API
- **Mar**: Steven prioritizes API, Henry wants PDF for sales demos
- **Apr**: Sarah hired, mediates: build PDF first (faster), API next
- **May**: API ships, ConsultPro happy, PrecisionEng wants webhooks
- **Jun**: Feature request backlog growing, need product manager

### Arc 4: Hiring Journey
- **Jan-Feb**: Just founders, overwhelmed
- **Mar**: Sarah joins as CSM, immediate relief for Henry
- **Apr**: Discuss hiring engineer, can't afford yet
- **May**: Steven burning out, seriously consider engineer hire
- **Jun**: Close to hiring offer, waiting on funding

### Arc 5: Funding Narrative
- **Jan**: Bootstrapped, $50k personal savings
- **Feb**: Revenue not covering costs, need funding
- **Mar**: Start talking to angels, mixed reception
- **Apr**: Lead investor interested, term sheet negotiations
- **May**: Term sheet received, debating valuation
- **Jun**: Close to signing, $500k seed round

### Arc 6: Client Drama
- **MegaConstruct Saga**: Signed Mar, heavy user, price increase in May caused near-churn, saved with grandfather pricing
- **PrecisionEng Power User**: Signed Apr, submits tons of feedback, pushes product forward but demanding
- **TinyBuilders Ghost**: Signed Mar, barely uses product, probably churning soon
- **BuildCorp Champion**: First client, evangelist, introduced UrbanDesign and EcoConstruct

## Ambiguities & Conflicts

1. **Pricing Strategy**: Henry wants predictable revenue (tiered), Steven prefers usage-based (fairer to clients)
2. **Architecture**: Steven wants to rebuild (engineer mindset), Henry wants to ship features (business mindset)
3. **MegaConstruct Value**: In customer calls they're enthusiastic, in support tickets they complain constantly
4. **Performance Claims**: Marketing site says "10x faster estimates" but actual client data shows 4-6x
5. **Feature Priority**: Every client wants something different, no clear consensus
6. **Burnout**: Steven says "I'm fine" in meetings but vents stress in Slack DMs
7. **Funding Valuation**: Henry thinks $3M is fair, Steven worried about dilution, investor offered $2.5M
